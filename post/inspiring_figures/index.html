Inspiring Figures

 Posted on February 3, 2021

For many years now I really enjoy following inspirational figures of our time. In this post I'd like take the time to highlight my personal favorites and show how they relate to each other.

Who are those inspiring figures?

  • Byron Katie
    • Highlight: Wisdom 2.0 Practice Day with Byron Katie
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Do You Punish Your Partner? How To Break Toxic Patterns | Russell Brand & Byron Katie
  • Saadhguru
    • Highlight: Sadhguru at TED Conference 2009
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Under the shin with Russel brand - Special guest Sadhguru
    • Interviewed by Tony Robbins: link no longer available… Saadhguru once visited Tony Robbins on his island. Instagram photo: https://www.instagram.com/p/BwBWQ4nHr4j/
  • Mooji
    • Highlight: A Guided Meditation with Mooji & Russell Brand
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Access The Awareness Beyond | Russell Brand & Mooji
  • Eckhart Tolle
    • Highlight: Living with Meaning, Purpose, and Wisdom in the Digital Age | Eckhart Tolle | Talks at Google
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Become Awake Now! | Eckhart Tolle & Russell Brand - Full Episode
  • Wim Hof
    • Highlight: BECOMING SUPERHUMAN WITH ICE MAN - Wim Hof
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Heal Yourself with The Ice Shaman | Wim Hof & Russell Brand
    • Interviewed by Saadhguru: “The Iceman” Wim Hof in Conversation With Sadhguru | Sadhguru Special
    • Interviewed by Jordan Peterson: The Power of the Cold With Wim Hof
  • Jordan Peterson
    • Highlight: Jordan Peterson debate on the gender pay gap, campus protests and postmodernism
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Russell Brand & Jordan Peterson - Kindness VS Power | Under The Skin #46
  • Tony Robbins
    • Interviewed by Russel Brand: Self-Realisation with Tony Robbins & Russell Brand
    • Highlights:
      • Tony Robbins Transforms an old lady
      • Tony Robbins Saves a suicidal person in less than 5 minutes | Tony Robbins | I am not your guru

All of these figures I knew before watching Russel Brand, but after stumbling over his channel I was surprised to see that he actually interviewed them all. I appreciate his efforts in making these people more known and interviewing them from his unique viewpoint. All of these figures can give people tools to better their lifes, which I think is infinitely valuable for everybody watching.

I also found his own series The Trews to be very interesting and entertaining. It gives a new perspective on the ordinary news and western politics.
