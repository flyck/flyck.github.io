Nouns and Verbs

 Posted on May 1, 2020

In the western societies we are very much goal oriented. We give each other goals and commandments, demanding for them to be fulfilled. You go figure out how to do that, after all, you can. It just matters whether you will. The peak of this sentiment really is Shia LaBeouf's “Just do it". We may laugh about such extremes, but one way or another, this goal orientation is everywhere in our society.

Listening to westerners giving advice can be quite comical. They really give each other commandments, together with their own success story, to prove that the commandment works. It is in this setting that people lose personal touch with each other. They share their ideals (similar to commandments) with each other, and see if the other person can still nod their head and be cheerful about it.

Can you recall yourself ever taking part in such a conversation?

A goal without a tool to reach it must introduce stress, unless you are blindly confident. If somebody is not shook by that next commandment you throw at him, it displays his sense of blind confidence. The way is not clear, but confidence is there. It is this that we teach each other about the way of doing things: be confident.

It is the subliminal teaching behind these conversations. Are you confident? Well, you aren't? Well, then I'm disappointed in you. If you are, well of course I am also, so we can agree and move on. In all this process, never is a detail of the actual process shared.

There are people agreeing about blind confidence and their achievements, and there are the others who “don't get it”.

It is in this context that I've been thinking about “nouns vs verbs”.

It is very useful to in given a conversation with a westerner ask this question: Am I being given a noun here (a goal / commandment/ ideal), or a verb (a tool how to get there). If it's the former, the other person is subliminally just asking for confidence. There is nothing else to respond with, because the actual subject of the matter is not being discussed.

If you are sensible enough to realize the stress which comes up with that new commandment, it may soothe you to know that this is normal. In this situation you can either:

  1. react with blind confidence
  2. realize what is going on and take a step back

On a deeper level you are not really discussing the matter at hand, but whether you can display confidence. That is a very easy thing to do. Of course you can be confident. But the actual tool of how to get to the goal tool will need to be forged later, possibly with somebody else, who is willing to share his process of doing things.

It is in this context that you will always be able to connect with people if you share your tools, your verbs, your way. The other intuitively knows that you are refraining from bullshitting him and yourself.

You are superficially and subliminally discussing the same thing. There are no two things going on at the same time. There is no bullshitting. It is this kind of conversation that brings calmness, peace, and displays your integrity. It also leaves room for joy, exploration and heartfelt discussions.

Give somebody a tool and you may have a friend. Give somebody a goal or commandment and you may have a sparring partner.
